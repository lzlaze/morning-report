# MORNING BRIEFING — Friday, March 06, 2026

---

## 1. OVERNIGHT SUMMARY

Broad risk-off selling accelerated overnight with crude oil spiking nearly 8% — likely driven by a geopolitical supply shock or escalation in the Middle East — which is dragging equities lower across the board. VIX has surged above 27.80, up 17%, signaling genuine fear, not just hedging. Bonds are modestly offered despite the equity selloff, suggesting the market is pricing stagflationary risk (higher oil + growth concerns) rather than a pure flight-to-quality deflationary scare.

---

## 2. ES vs YM DIVERGENCE ANALYSIS

ES (-1.29%) and YM (-1.33%) are moving nearly in lockstep — no meaningful divergence. This is a broad macro-driven selloff, not a rotation story. The slight YM underperformance makes sense: Dow-heavy industrials (CAT, BA, HON) and transports get hit directly by energy cost spikes, while financials (-0.52%) are a larger Dow weight and are also under pressure. Tech's relative outperformance in the sector data (+0.24%) is actually cushioning ES marginally vs YM, but the difference is negligible. The real outlier is RTY at -2.31% — small caps are getting crushed as higher input costs and tighter financial conditions disproportionately hit smaller, less-hedged companies. This is a correlated selloff — trade them as a pair, not a spread today.

## 5. KNOW BEFORE YOU GO

The dominant theme is stagflation risk — crude ripping 8% while equities dump and bonds fail to rally is the textbook stagflation tape, and that's the worst environment for the Fed to act. The 8:30 AM jobs report is the landmine: if payrolls come in hot with wage acceleration, expect ES to test below 6,700 as the market prices out any remaining 2026 rate cuts. Watch crude oil as your lead indicator today — if WTI stabilizes or reverses, equities likely find a floor; if it pushes toward $90, all bets are off and we're looking at a potential 2%+ down day. A soft jobs print with cooling wages would be the only catalyst that could flip the tape, giving the Fed cover to signal easing despite the energy shock. Position for two-way volatility around 8:30, not directional conviction.
