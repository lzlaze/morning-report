## 2. ES vs YM DIVERGENCE ANALYSIS

Clear divergence. YM is significantly outperforming ES this morning (-0.51% vs -0.89%), and NQ is the tell.

The math here is straightforward: NQ is down 1.43%, and tech/mega-cap growth represents ~30% of the S&P 500 but a much smaller slice of the Dow 30. The Dow is insulated because its top weights skew toward UnitedHealth, Goldman, Microsoft (yes, but less concentrated), Caterpillar, Amex — names tied to financials and industrials, which are the leading sectors today (Financials +1.21%, Industrials +0.63%). That sector strength is actively cushioning YM while ES gets dragged by its tech overweight.

Russell at only -0.25% reinforces this: small caps (domestic, value-tilted) are shrugging it off. This is a large-cap growth problem, not a broad market problem. If you're trading YM today, respect the relative strength — it's structural, not noise. The spread could narrow if tech finds a bid after the open, but for now, YM longs are the cleaner side if you need to be in something.
