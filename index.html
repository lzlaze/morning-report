# MORNING BRIEFING — Thursday, March 05, 2026

---

## 1. OVERNIGHT SUMMARY

Broad risk-off session overnight with equities selling hard across the board, led by small caps (RTY -1.36%) and Dow (YM -1.25%). Crude oil surging +5.37% suggests a geopolitical supply shock or escalation — likely Middle East or Russia-related — which is pressuring bonds (30Y -0.98%) on stagflation fears while VIX pops above 22. Gold pulling back modestly despite the risk-off tone is notable and may reflect margin/liquidity-driven selling or dollar strength.

## 2. ES vs YM DIVERGENCE ANALYSIS

YM is significantly underperforming ES by ~62 bps — meaningful divergence. The Dow's heavier weighting toward industrials, financials, and energy-consuming conglomerates makes it more vulnerable to a crude oil shock. Financials (-0.80%) and Materials (-1.18%) are getting hit hard — both are overweight in YM vs ES. Meanwhile, Technology (+0.22%) is the only sector showing any bid, which is cushioning ES and especially NQ (-0.43%). The energy spike is a double-edged sword: it helps XOM/CVX (Dow components) but the net effect of higher input costs hammering industrials like CAT, HON, and BA is clearly winning. This is a classic stagflation-fear rotation where ES holds up better on mega-cap tech resilience while YM eats the damage from the old-economy drag.
