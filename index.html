## 2. ES vs YM DIVERGENCE ANALYSIS

ES and YM are locked in step at -1.12% each — no meaningful divergence. This is notable because you'd expect NQ (-1.38%) and RTY (-1.72%) to underperform in an oil shock / stagflation scare, and they are. The fact that YM isn't outperforming ES tells you the Dow's heavier industrial and financial weighting isn't providing a cushion — rising energy input costs hit industrials (CAT, HON, BA) just as hard as the margin compression threat hits broader SPX. Energy names in both indices (CVX in YM, XOM/CVX in SPX) are providing identical offsetting support. The real story is the NQ-ES spread: tech's extra 30bps of underperformance reflects growth/duration sensitivity to the implied rate repricing — if oil stays here, the Fed isn't cutting anytime soon. RTY getting hammered hardest (-1.72%) is textbook: small caps can't absorb energy cost pass-through.
