# MORNING BRIEFING — Friday, March 06, 2026

---

## 1. OVERNIGHT SUMMARY

Broad, aggressive risk-off selling across all equity indices overnight with small caps (RTY -2.47%) leading the carnage — this is a liquidation move, not a rotation. Crude oil spiking +9.4% to $88.64 points to a geopolitical supply shock (likely Middle East escalation or sanctions enforcement) which is simultaneously hammering growth expectations and driving VIX to $28.35 (+19%). Gold bid modestly, bonds surprisingly weak (-0.86% on 30Y), suggesting the market is pricing stagflation — higher energy costs AND sticky inflation keeping the Fed pinned.

---

## 2. ES vs YM DIVERGENCE ANALYSIS

YM is underperforming ES by ~33bps (-1.93% vs -1.60%), which is meaningful. The Dow's heavier weighting toward industrials, transports, and energy-consuming manufacturers (CAT, HON, BA, MMM) makes it acutely sensitive to an oil shock — input costs spike immediately for these names. Financials in the Dow also get hit on recession fear repricing. ES is holding up marginally better because mega-cap tech (AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, META) — which dominates S&P weighting — is relatively more insulated from direct energy cost pass-through in the near term. Note that NQ at -1.47% confirms tech is the relative shelter trade today. RTY getting destroyed at -2.47% tells you credit/recession risk is what's really being priced — small caps can't absorb an energy shock.

## 4. ECONOMIC DATA IMPACT

| Time | Event | Impact |
|------|-------|--------|
| 8:30 AM ET | BLS Release — Likely Nonfarm Payrolls (Jobs Report) | 🔴 HIGH |

This is almost certainly the February NFP report. First Friday of the month + BLS + 8:30 AM = payrolls day. This is the single highest-impact data point possible on a day where the market is already pricing stagflation. A hot number (strong jobs + strong wages) is the worst outcome — it confirms the Fed can't cut into an oil shock, and equities go lower. A weak number accelerates recession pricing and likely deepens the selloff too, though bonds might finally catch a bid. The goldilocks scenario is modest payroll growth with cooling wages — that's the only thing that gives bulls any room. This print will define the session.

---

## 5. KNOW BEFORE YOU GO

The dominant theme is stagflation repricing driven by the crude oil shock — full stop. The 8:30 AM jobs report is the binary event that determines whether this selloff stabilizes or accelerates into a flush; watch the wage component (AHE) even more than the headline number, because that's what tells you if the Fed's hands are truly tied. ES has key support at 6,700 (round number + prior consolidation zone) — a break below opens 6,650 fast with this VIX level. What changes my bias from bearish to neutral: oil reverses below $84, VIX fails to hold 27, AND the jobs report comes in soft on wages — all three, not one. This is a Friday with elevated vol heading into the weekend; expect dealers to be short gamma, meaning moves get amplified in both directions. Respect the tape, keep size appropriate.
